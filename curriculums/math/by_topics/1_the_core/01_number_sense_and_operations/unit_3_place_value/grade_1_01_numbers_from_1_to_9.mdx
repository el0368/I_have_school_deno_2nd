## ðŸ“ The Number Line 1 to 9

A **number line** shows numbers in order from left to right.
Numbers to the **right** are always **bigger**.
Numbers to the **left** are always **smaller**.

$$1 \;-\; 2 \;-\; 3 \;-\; 4 \;-\; 5 \;-\; 6 \;-\; 7 \;-\; 8 \;-\; 9$$

- Going **right** (+1 each step) means getting **larger**: $3 \rightarrow 4 \rightarrow 5$
- Going **left** (âˆ’1 each step) means getting **smaller**: $7 \rightarrow 6 \rightarrow 5$

### Before, After, and Between

| Relationship | How to find it | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Before** (one less) | Move **one step left** | The number before $6$ is $6 - 1 = 5$ |
| **After** (one more) | Move **one step right** | The number after $6$ is $6 + 1 = 7$ |
| **Between** | Sits in the middle of two neighbors | $6$ is between $5$ and $7$ |

**Try it:** What number is between $3$ and $5$?
> $3 \;-\; ? \;-\; 5$
> Answer: $4$, because $3 < 4 < 5$.

## âš–ï¸ Comparing Numbers: Greater, Less, or Equal?

We use three symbols when comparing two numbers:

| Symbol | Read as | Meaning | Example | Read aloud |
|:---:|---|---|---|---|
| $>$ | "is greater than" | Left number is **bigger** | $7 > 4$ | "Seven is greater than four" |
| $<$ | "is less than" | Left number is **smaller** | $2 < 6$ | "Two is less than six" |
| $=$ | "is equal to" | Both numbers are the **same** | $5 = 5$ | "Five is equal to five" |

> ðŸŠ **Alligator trick:** Imagine the symbol is a hungry alligator's mouth.  
> The alligator **always eats the bigger number** â€” its mouth opens toward the larger side!  
> $3 < 8$ â€” the mouth opens toward $8$ (the bigger meal).  
> $9 > 2$ â€” the mouth opens toward $9$.

### Step-by-Step: How to Compare Two Numbers

**Compare $6$ and $4$:**

1. Find both numbers on the number line.
2. $6$ is to the **right** of $4$.
3. Therefore $6$ is **greater**: $\;6 > 4$.

**Compare $3$ and $7$:**

1. Find both numbers on the number line.
2. $3$ is to the **left** of $7$.
3. Therefore $3$ is **less**: $\;3 < 7$.

## ðŸŒŸ Odd and Even: A First Peek

Numbers from $1$ to $9$ split into two families:

| Family | Numbers | Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| **Even** | $2, \quad 4, \quad 6, \quad 8$ | Can be split into **two equal groups** |
| **Odd** | $1, \quad 3, \quad 5, \quad 7, \quad 9$ | Always **one left over** when split in two |

**Example â€” Is $6$ even or odd?**

$$6 = 3 + 3 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{two equal groups of } 3 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \textbf{even}$$

**Example â€” Is $5$ even or odd?**

$$5 = 2 + 2 + 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{one is left over} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \textbf{odd}$$
